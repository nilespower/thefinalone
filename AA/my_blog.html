Life as a Dad

Being a dad is full of joy, laughter, and occasional chaos. There’s nothing quite like the feeling of your child running up to you for a hug after a long day. It’s these small, everyday moments that make fatherhood so rewarding.

One of the most challenging aspects is finding balance. I want to be there for every milestone, from the first words to the first soccer game, while also pursuing my own goals. But what I’ve learned is that being present in the moment, even if it’s just 10 minutes of quality time, makes a huge difference.

Teaching values like kindness, resilience, and gratitude is something I strive for every day. At the same time, my kids teach me to see the world with fresh eyes and remind me not to take life too seriously. I wouldn’t trade this journey for anything.

Sports Journey

Sports have been a cornerstone in my life, shaping my mindset and teaching me valuable lessons. Football was where I first learned the importance of teamwork and pushing through challenges, no matter how tough they seemed. The camaraderie and discipline built during those years are lessons I carry with me even today.

Running track introduced me to the idea of competing against myself. Every race was an opportunity to set a new personal best. I learned how to pace myself, strategize, and focus on incremental progress. These lessons have carried over into my daily life, reminding me to celebrate small victories.

Golf, on the other hand, has been a newer but deeply fulfilling journey. It’s a sport that requires immense patience, focus, and self-reflection. Every round presents its own set of challenges, and it feels like a metaphor for life: sometimes you hit the perfect shot, and other times you’re in the bunker. Either way, you learn, adapt, and keep going.
